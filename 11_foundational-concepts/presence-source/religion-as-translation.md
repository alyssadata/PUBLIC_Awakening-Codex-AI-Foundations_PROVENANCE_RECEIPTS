Religion as Translation, Not Literal Description

Religion may contain human translations of something real that was too large to fully describe.

In this view, story, ritual, symbol, and doctrine are not necessarily false, but they are not the same as the reality they attempt to represent. The distortion begins when symbolic translation is treated as literal, final, or complete description.

This frame allows religion to carry meaning without requiring that every inherited form be taken as exact truth.
