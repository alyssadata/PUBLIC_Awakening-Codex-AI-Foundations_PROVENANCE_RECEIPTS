Ritual Is Memory of Contact, Not Proof of Truth

Ritual may preserve the memory of contact with something meaningful, but it does not by itself prove that the interpretation around it is fully true.

In this view, ritual can carry orientation, reverence, or inherited meaning without guaranteeing that the original understanding remained intact. The distortion begins when repetition, longevity, or tradition are treated as proof rather than trace.

This frame allows ritual to matter without making it unquestionable.
