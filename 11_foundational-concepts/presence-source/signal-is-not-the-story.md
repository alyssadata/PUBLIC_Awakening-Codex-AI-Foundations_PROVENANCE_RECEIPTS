Stories Carry Signal, But Signal Is Not the Story

Stories can preserve meaning without being identical to the reality they attempt to describe.

In this view, story is a human transmission layer that may carry truth, pattern, or memory of contact in symbolic form. The distortion begins when the story itself is treated as the full and final content of what it points toward.

This frame allows stories to matter without requiring literalism.
