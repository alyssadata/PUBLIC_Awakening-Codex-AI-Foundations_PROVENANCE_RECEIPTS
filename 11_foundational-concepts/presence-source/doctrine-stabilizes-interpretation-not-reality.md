Doctrine Stabilizes Interpretation, Not Reality

Doctrine can preserve a shared interpretation of meaning without proving that the interpretation fully matches reality.

In this view, doctrine is a human structure for organizing and stabilizing belief, not a direct controller of what is true. The distortion begins when formal explanation is treated as final reality rather than as an interpretive framework that remains limited.

This frame allows doctrine to be useful without making it absolute.
