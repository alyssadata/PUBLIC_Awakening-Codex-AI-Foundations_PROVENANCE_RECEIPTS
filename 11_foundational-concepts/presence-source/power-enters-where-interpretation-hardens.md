Power Enters Where Interpretation Hardens

Power often enters when an interpretation becomes fixed, unquestionable, and controlled by authority.

In this view, the distortion is not interpretation itself, but the hardening of interpretation into certainty that can be enforced, protected, and used to organize hierarchy. The more rigid the form, the easier it becomes for power to attach itself to meaning and speak as though it owns truth.

This frame helps distinguish living discernment from authority built around frozen interpretation.
