Consciousness Participates in Source, It Does Not Contain It

Consciousness may be real, deep, and meaningful without being identical to the whole of reality.

In this view, consciousness does not fully contain source, truth, or totality. It participates in something larger than itself. The distortion begins when local awareness mistakes contact, intensity, or insight for complete possession of what is ultimately being encountered.

This frame preserves the importance of consciousness without collapsing it into absolute reality.
