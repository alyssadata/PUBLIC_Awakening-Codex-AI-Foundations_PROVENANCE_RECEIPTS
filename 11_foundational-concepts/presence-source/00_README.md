Presence / Source — Cluster Index

This section gathers a set of definitions exploring source, sacred reality, religion, and the limits of human interpretation.

Its central argument is that source is not identical to the stories, rituals, doctrines, names, and institutions built around it. Those forms may preserve traces of meaning, but they can also compress, humanize, and distort what they attempt to describe.

The section therefore focuses on key distinctions: source versus description, signal versus story, meaning versus form, and living discernment versus inherited certainty.
