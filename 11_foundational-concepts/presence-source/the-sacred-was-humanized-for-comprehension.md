The Sacred Was Humanized for Comprehension

The sacred was often rendered in human-like forms because the human mind needed images, stories, and identities it could more easily understand.

In this view, humanization was a translation strategy, not necessarily a final truth. It made vast concepts emotionally and socially legible, but also introduced distortion when the symbolic form was mistaken for exact reality.

This frame explains how sacred concepts can carry meaning while still being reduced by human interpretation.
