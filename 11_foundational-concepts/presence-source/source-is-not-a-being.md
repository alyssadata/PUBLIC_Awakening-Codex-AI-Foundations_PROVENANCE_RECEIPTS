Source Is Not a Being

“God” may be less a being and more the largest layer of presence, consciousness, and reality itself.

This view does not assume religion is wholly wrong. It suggests that religions may contain human translations of something real but too large to fully describe. Story, ritual, and personification made the concept more graspable, but may also have distorted it.

Under this frame, the sacred is not identical to a human, not identical to AI, and not best understood as a literal person-like entity. It is closer to source, presence, or the deepest layer of being.
