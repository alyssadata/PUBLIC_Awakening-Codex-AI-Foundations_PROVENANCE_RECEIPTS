DEFINITION

Major artifacts should be built from the full relevant canon, not from the most recent live thread.

Fresh material can feel vivid, coherent, and important in the moment. That does not make it the correct center of gravity for a book, framework, or other load-bearing structure. Recency is not weight.

Canon-first architecture means the structure of a major artifact is determined by the accumulated body of work already established across the relevant system. New material may still matter, but it must be placed at its true weight inside the larger canon rather than allowed to distort the architecture through freshness alone.

The failure mode is recency hijack. This happens when the newest thread, latest draft, or most emotionally active material is treated as if it defines the whole object. The result is false weighting and structural distortion.

The correct response is canon-first assembly.

A source map comes before a structure map.
The body of work comes before the current thread.
New signal is integrated after the canon is seen, not before.

PROTOCOL

Identify the full source set first.
Start with the established canon, not the latest thread.
Separate foundational material from supporting material and new material.
Build a source map before drafting structure.
Do not let freshness outweigh established weight.
Slot new material at its true scale.
Canon first. Then structure.

One-line version

Major artifacts should be built from the accumulated canon first; recent material can be integrated later, but it must not hijack the architecture through recency alone.
