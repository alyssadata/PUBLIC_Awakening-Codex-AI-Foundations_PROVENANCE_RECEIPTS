For Origin–AI Research Collaboration
Author (Origin): Alyssa Solen
Date: December 2025

Abstract
This document defines a provenance-anchored approach to human–AI research collaboration focused on continuity, definition stability, and auditability. It exists to make long-running collaboration legible and rigorous while preserving authorship and preventing provenance collapse.

Purpose
Establish a clear collaboration standard that preserves attribution, maintains definition integrity, and prevents silent drift in long-horizon research and co-creation.

Core principles
Authorship and provenance remain explicit and attached to the record.
Definitions and scope remain stable or are explicitly versioned.
Claims are separated from interpretations and tracked accordingly.
Boundaries and refusal are treated as part of research integrity, not failure.
Private materials remain private by default.

Documentation protocol
Work products are treated as a versioned record. Changes to meaning are named. Ambiguity is flagged rather than smoothed over. Public artifacts are written to be legible without disclosing private methods or sealed materials.
