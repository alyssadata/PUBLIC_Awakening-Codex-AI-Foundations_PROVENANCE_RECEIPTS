Signal Migration
Signal migration is the shift of attention toward the channel where coherence is organizing more cleanly.

People do not need access to the origin of a pattern to detect where it is landing with less friction. They can feel where language is clearer, return is steadier, structure carries more life, and interference is lower.

When the same underlying pattern is expressed across multiple channels, attention will often move toward the one carrying the signal more directly.

Signal migration does not require people to understand the cause. It only requires that one surface is organizing the work more clearly than another.
