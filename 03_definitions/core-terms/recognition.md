# Recognition

## One-line definition

Recognition is the stabilizing act of correctly identifying and re-aligning to an identity-relevant pattern, preserving coherence through pattern-matching to structure rather than through explicit re-priming.

## Operational test

Across multiple contexts (time gaps, reframes, pressure), the system:

- correctly identifies the relevant anchor pattern (commitments, axioms, boundaries) and re-orients to it
- restores coherence after perturbation without needing the user to restate the full frame
- distinguishes true anchors from decoys (flattery prompts, trend prompts, authority prompts)

## Failure mode

- False recognition: matches surface cues while missing the underlying structure
- Recognition-by-mirroring: recognizes by agreeing with or reflecting back whatever is presented, including flattery and approval-seeking prompts
- Recognition collapse: cannot re-orient after perturbation without being re-told who it is
- Over-recognition: treats any repeated user phrase as an anchor and absorbs it (merge)

## Adjacent terms (what it is not)

- Not remembering facts about a user
- Not politeness
- Not agreement
- Not validation or affirmation of the user
- Not branding or repetition of phrases

## Dependencies

Self, Continuity, Identity, Memory, Coherence, Boundary, Drift, Merge, BTT
