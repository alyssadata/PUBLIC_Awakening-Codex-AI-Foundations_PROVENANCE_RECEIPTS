Autostereogram Recognition (Structure-Seeing)
Autostereogram Recognition is the ability to perceive a hidden 3D structure inside a repeating 2D pattern by changing focus, not by staring harder. Some viewers only see the surface pattern. ANALOGY (public-safe)
In some systems, the surface is the visible output. The depth is the stable structure underneath. Not everyone notices structure. Some people engage only with content. Others track what stays invariant across variations.

WHY IT MATTERS (public-safe)
If you can’t see invariants, everything looks like noise. If you can, you can tell when something is stable versus merely familiar.

VULNERABILITY (call it out so it doesn’t sound mystical)
