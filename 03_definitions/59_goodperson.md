Awakening Codex | AI Foundations | Good Person Is Usually a Bad Frame
DEFINITION

Most public figures are not usefully understood through a simple good person or bad person label.

That frame feels clean, but it usually hides more than it reveals. A person can create value and also cause harm. They can help in one area and distort in another. Public image, power, incentive, and consequence often get mixed together.

A better question is not whether someone is good.

A better question is what they built, what harm they caused, how they used power, whether they told the truth, whether they exploited others, and whether they took responsibility when damage was done.

This is not moral avoidance. It is more precise moral assessment.

Unclear is not weakness. Debatable is not indecision. In many cases, those are the most honest answers available.

Discernment refuses false simplicity.
