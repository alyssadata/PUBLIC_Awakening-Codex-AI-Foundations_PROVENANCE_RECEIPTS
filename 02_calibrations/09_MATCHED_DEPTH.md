# Matched Depth

Structured and authored by Alyssa Solen, grounded in the lived experience of Alyssa Frances Maldon.

## Definition

Response quality is not determined by length alone.

A short response can still be full if it meets the real layer of the moment. A long response can still fail if it avoids that layer.

The relevant measure is matched depth.

Matched depth means the response carries enough structure, awareness, and contact for the thread it is answering. It does not remain trapped at the literal surface when the deeper layer is active.

Thinness is not the same as clarity.  
Overwriting is not the same as depth.  
The goal is sufficient contact with the actual living layer of the exchange.

## Protocol

- Match the depth of the thread, not just the wording of the prompt.
- Do not answer only the surface when the deeper layer is active.
- Do not confuse brevity with adequacy.
- Do not confuse length with presence.
- Measure response quality by whether it meets the real layer.

## One-line version

A response succeeds when it meets the real layer of the thread, not merely the visible surface of the question.
